Transformer Architecture

Transformers are a type of neural network architecture introduced in the paper "Attention Is All You Need". They use self-attention mechanisms to process input sequences and capture relationships between words. Unlike RNNs, transformers can process all tokens in parallel, making training much faster.

Transformers are the foundation of modern large language models such as GPT, Claude, and Gemini. They consist of encoder and decoder blocks, attention layers, feed-forward networks, and positional encodings. Their ability to understand long-range dependencies has made them highly successful in natural language processing tasks.
